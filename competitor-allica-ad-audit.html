Biggest Choke Point

Alignment — The ad promise and the landing page are talking about different things

Every Allica Meta ad promises: "Up to 4.08% AER + 1.5% cashback + no monthly fees." The landing page headline says: "Explore our business bank account." There is no rate, no cashback, no reason to stay. A business owner who clicked expecting to see those numbers will spend the first 5 seconds asking "is this the right page?" — and leave. This is the single biggest lever in their entire system.

OakNorth Interview Intelligence — What This Means
Strategic Takeaways for Interview & BD Outreach
  • Allica's paid acquisition is rate-only. Every ad is a rate comparison. There is no brand story, no sector expertise, no relationship narrative in any Meta or LinkedIn ad. OakNorth's opportunity is sector-specific acquisition — "property CFOs who hold cash between completions" — which Allica's generic rate play cannot match.
  • The local banker model doesn't scale through paid channels. Allica's famous local banker advantage (their competitive moat in the research brief) is absent from paid media. The LinkedIn strategy is literally one banker (Matt West) with an email address. OakNorth running sector-targeted LinkedIn campaigns with a proper landing page would directly out-execute this.
  • Their acquisition system has broken links in it right now. Traffic is being sent to fee schedule and T&C pages. LinkedIn has a live ad with a broken %FIRSTNAME% token. These are not strategic weaknesses — they're operational failures. They signal a marketing function that is executing but not reviewing.
  • FSCS change is not being used as an acquisition trigger by Allica — despite being one of their most relevant moments. No Meta ad mentions FSCS. This is a window OakNorth can enter before Allica figures it out.
  • Allica's rate advantage is real — OakNorth needs to answer it, not ignore it. Allica advertises 4.08–4.33% AER (including temporary boosts: 0.5% ongoing, 0.5% CASS switch for 6 months, 0.25% welcome for 3 months) against OakNorth's 3.35% AER instant access. The correct interview answer is not rate parity — it is: sector expertise (Allica ads are completely undifferentiated across all sectors), a combined lending and deposit relationship (CFOs consolidate banking with their lender), Monite bank-native AP/AR automation (Allica has no equivalent), and OakNorth's 10-year clean regulatory record. The CFO choosing OakNorth over Allica is choosing the relationship and the product ecosystem. Verify live rates at oaknorth.co.uk/business-savings and allica.bank/business-savings before the interview.
What Allica Should Fix (OakNorth's Counterplay)
1
Fix the landing page headline — match the ad promise above the fold
Replace "Explore our business bank account" with "4.08% AER. 1.5% cashback. No monthly fees." — exact match to what the ad promised. For OakNorth: build a landing page that mirrors whatever the CFO just read in the outreach email.
2
Segment by sector — stop showing one ad to everyone
Three campaigns: property (cash between completions), professional services (client money compliance), hospitality (trading account optimisation). Each with a sector-specific headline and landing page. OakNorth's head start here is their sector data — they know which sectors have the most cash.
3
Add a low-friction CTA path — calculator before application
"See how much your reserves could earn" → yield calculator → then Apply Now. Instead of sending a cold prospect straight to "Apply Now" (a high-commitment step), qualify them with a calculator that shows the rate gap. This is the exact acquisition mechanic OakNorth's landing page mockup uses.
4
Use the FSCS change as a time-sensitive acquisition trigger
Allica has the same FSCS protection window OakNorth does — they're not using it. A Meta campaign "Your deposits are now protected up to £120,000 — but only if you act before [date]" would generate switching intent. OakNorth should run this before Allica wakes up to it.
