Headline Finding

Starling Business has almost no B2B paid acquisition activity visible in May 2026.

3 genuine Starling Business ads found in Meta Ad Library. Zero LinkedIn ads. No landing pages captured. The ads that were found are consumer-adjacent (freelancer UGC content, Which? award badge) — not targeted at the SME CFOs OakNorth competes for. Starling is not actively acquiring OakNorth's target customers through paid channels right now.

Trust Risk — £29M FCA Fine (2025)

Starling Bank received a £29 million FCA fine in 2025 for financial crime prevention failures. For a CFO evaluating where to hold company reserves — FSCS protection, regulatory compliance, and bank reputation are threshold requirements. This fine is the single largest reputational liability Starling carries into any CFO conversation. OakNorth has zero equivalent regulatory incidents over its 10-year history and a 0.045% cumulative loss rate. This is a direct positioning advantage in any deposit acquisition conversation.

Biggest Structural Gap

Starling is not competing for OakNorth's customers in paid media at all

At OakNorth's lending threshold (£500k to £45M), Starling is largely below the minimum. Their product is designed for smaller SMEs and freelancers — the ads confirm this (UGC creator content, freelancer finance). The competitive overlap with OakNorth is on current accounts for smaller businesses, not on bespoke lending or high-balance savings. This is a moderate, not critical, competitive threat — and their paid acquisition confirms it.

£29M FCA Fine Creates a Trust Gap OakNorth Can Name
Starling received a £29 million FCA fine in 2025 for financial crime prevention failures. A CFO responsible for a company's banking relationship cannot ignore this. When Starling's Which? badge says "seventh year as a recommended provider" — the FCA fine is the other side of that story. OakNorth's 10-year regulatory clean record and 0.045% cumulative loan loss rate is a direct competitive differentiator in any conversation with a CFO who heard about the fine.

High — Positioning
Starling's Paid Messaging Targets Freelancers, Not OakNorth's ICP
The UGC ad features a creative/freelancer talking about personal finance. The product ad ("no monthly fees") appeals to very small businesses. Starling's sweet spot is sub-£2M businesses — companies that have outgrown their Starling Business account by the time they reach OakNorth's minimum thresholds. Starling is OakNorth's feeder market, not its primary competitor. Companies graduate from Starling to OakNorth, not the other way around.
Ad copy: "Organising my finances with @starlingbank has helped me to be so much better with money, which is so important as a freelancer." — Personal finance UGC, not SME CFO targeting.
Medium — Acquisition
Feature-Led Messaging ("No Monthly Fees") Competes on Price, Not Outcome
"A business bank account with no monthly fees and free accountancy software included. Don't pay for what you don't need." This is a cost-reduction message for small businesses watching every £. A CFO at a £10M company deciding where to place £500k+ in reserves is not motivated by "no monthly fees" — they're motivated by yield, security, and relationship quality. Starling's message is structurally misaligned with OakNorth's ICP.
Positive (for Starling) — Credibility
Which? Recommended Provider for 7 Consecutive Years
The Which? badge is independently earned and credible. It signals that Starling's product is well-reviewed by a consumer watchdog. However, this accolade is for the banking product overall — not specifically for the deposit safety, rate quality, or lending relationship that OakNorth's ICP values most. The FCA fine directly undermines the trust signal this badge is trying to build.

OakNorth Interview Intelligence — Combined Competitor Picture
What Three Audits Tell You About OakNorth's Acquisition Window
  • LinkedIn is completely uncontested. Allica has one banker's email address. Revolut has EU-language ads that don't reach UK CFOs. Starling has nothing. OakNorth running targeted LinkedIn campaigns at Finance Directors and CFOs by sector would be the only professional-channel paid acquisition from any challenger bank in the UK right now. This is the most important insight from the three audits combined.
  • No competitor is using FSCS as an acquisition trigger. Allica mentions it on landing pages. Revolut doesn't use their March 2026 licence in any ad. Starling has nothing. The December 2025 FSCS limit change to £120,000 — the most significant banking policy change affecting CFO deposit decisions in years — has produced zero paid acquisition campaigns from any of these three banks. This is OakNorth's to own.
  • No competitor uses sector language in any ad. Not one ad from Allica, Revolut, or Starling calls out a property company, a professional services firm, or a hospitality group. Every CFO in those sectors sees a generic banking ad. OakNorth's sector research (property, professional services, hospitality) is a structural advantage that none of the competitors are using in paid media.
  • Starling's FCA fine is an OakNorth talking point. In a CFO conversation about where to bank, "we have zero FCA enforcement actions in ten years, a 0.045% cumulative loss rate, and we are FCA and PRA regulated" directly contrasts with Starling's £29M fine. This is not disparagement — it's a regulatory clean record, which is a threshold requirement for a CFO holding company cash.
  • Allica's acquisition is rate competition — it collapses if OakNorth matches the rate. Allica's entire paid strategy is "4.08% AER, 1.5% cashback, no fees." If OakNorth runs sector-specific campaigns with a competitive savings rate and a relationship banking story, Allica has nothing left to say beyond "us too." OakNorth's AI credit intelligence and Monite finance automation are differentiators Allica cannot match.

1

Sector-specific LinkedIn campaigns targeting Finance Directors (property) and CFOs (professional services). Zero paid competition from Allica, Revolut, or Starling on this channel. OakNorth would be the only challenger bank speaking directly to B2B decision-makers on LinkedIn.
2

"Your reserves are now FSCS-protected up to £120,000. 3.35% AER. Next-day access." None of the three competitors are saying this in paid media. The window opened December 2025. OakNorth is the first mover if they move now.
3
Use sector language in every ad — no competitor does this at all
Three ad sets: "For property companies between completions." "For professional services firms holding client money." "For hospitality groups with seasonal cash." Every CFO in those sectors currently sees only generic banking ads. Sector-specific copy attracts buyers, not browsers.
4
Position the regulatory clean record explicitly
Against Starling's FCA fine: OakNorth's 0.045% cumulative loss rate and zero enforcement actions in 10 years is the strongest CFO trust signal in the challenger bank space. This belongs in the ad copy, not just on the landing page.
