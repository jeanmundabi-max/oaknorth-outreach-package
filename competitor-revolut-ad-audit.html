Strategic Intelligence for OakNorth

Revolut is NOT advertising deposits or savings in the UK. Their FSCS licence is invisible in their paid media.

Despite getting their full UK banking licence in March 2026 — making them FSCS-protected for the first time — Revolut is running zero UK deposit or savings acquisition campaigns. Their paid media is entirely FX, international payments, and account management. OakNorth's deposit acquisition window is open right now. Revolut's 800,000 business customers have not been actively pushed toward treating Revolut as their primary savings vehicle.

Biggest Choke Point

Alignment — Revolut's landing page is about app features. A CFO looking for somewhere to park £500k doesn't see a rate, a yield comparison, or an FSCS mention.

The primary Revolut Business landing page: "This is business banking. The all-in-one business account." No rate. No FSCS. No savings product. It's an acquisition page for current account sign-ups, not for deposit movement. A CFO who clicked "high exchange markups" ad arrives to a page about bank account features — misaligned from the start.

OakNorth Interview Intelligence — What This Means
Strategic Takeaways for Interview & BD Outreach
  • The "Revolut threat" in the research brief is real but not yet expressed in paid media. The threat is passive — existing Revolut customers staying with Revolut for deposits now that FSCS applies — not active acquisition. Revolut is not running campaigns to pull CFOs away from OakNorth. They're relying on inertia, not outbound. OakNorth's outbound channel would be the first mover in actively targeting the switching moment.
  • LinkedIn is an open field for OakNorth. No UK LinkedIn campaigns from Revolut. No UK LinkedIn campaigns from Allica (their LinkedIn is one banker's email). If OakNorth runs sector-specific LinkedIn campaigns targeting Finance Directors and CFOs, they have zero paid competition from the two most relevant challengers. This is the exact argument for why LinkedIn acquisition investment has a high return right now.
  • Revolut's 800,000 customers are not being told to use Revolut for savings. The passive threat: Revolut will capture deposits from CFOs who bank there already and notice the FSCS notification. The active counter: OakNorth reaching those same CFOs with a specific rate comparison ("You're on Revolut. Here's what your reserves could earn elsewhere.") before Revolut runs that campaign themselves.
  • FX is Revolut's moat — don't compete there. "Better international payments" is Revolut's message and it owns that space. OakNorth's deposit acquisition messaging should never compete on FX. OakNorth's angle is yield on cash reserves and sector expertise — completely different buyer motivation.
  • Revolut will eventually run a deposits campaign. Getting the licensing was the hard part. Running a "your deposits are now FSCS protected — earn X% AER" campaign is a marketing decision they haven't made yet. OakNorth's six-to-twelve month window closes the day Revolut turns that campaign on. The current audit finds they haven't started yet as of May 2026.
What OakNorth Should Do While Revolut Is Asleep
1
Run LinkedIn campaigns now — the channel is completely open
Finance Directors and CFOs on LinkedIn are not seeing a single UK business banking deposit campaign from Revolut or Allica. OakNorth running sector-specific LinkedIn ads (property CFOs, professional services FDs) with a specific rate and FSCS message would be the only player in that channel.
2
Target Revolut business customers directly — before Revolut does
A CFO who banks on Revolut for operations has just received the FSCS protection notification. They're asking "where should my reserves sit?" OakNorth can answer that question before Revolut runs their own campaign. Target: businesses using Revolut for international payments, looking for somewhere better for their GBP cash reserves.
3
Use the FSCS window as the explicit switching trigger
Revolut has not used their FSCS licence to run a UK deposit campaign. OakNorth's angle: "FSCS-protected up to £120,000. 3.35% AER. Next-day access." The switching trigger is there — the CFO just needs to know OakNorth is offering it. Revolut's silence on deposits is OakNorth's loudest opportunity.
