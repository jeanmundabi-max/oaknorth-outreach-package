5-Pillar GTM Strategy

Each pillar addresses a distinct acquisition gap in OakNorth's current Business Banking model.

Pillar 1

FSCS Tailwind Sprint

Target UK SME CFOs and FDs with operating cash above £100,000 at incumbent banks. Lead with the December 2025 FSCS change and OakNorth's Notice and Fixed Term rates (3.62–3.80% AER) versus the incumbent average of ~1.5%.

  • 25-company targeting database built from Companies House
  • Trigger email: FSCS window + Revolut threat
  • Goal: 8 to 12 qualified deposit conversations
Pillar 2

Monite Product Launch

Target Finance Directors at companies currently posting for Accounts Payable or Accounts Receivable roles. OakNorth's bank-native automation eliminates the headcount they are about to hire.

  • 23+ companies identified on Indeed in the last 30 days
  • Angle: bank-native AP/AR versus Starling + Xero workflow
  • Goal: 8 to 12 qualified Monite conversations
Pillar 3

NACFB Broker Activation

Put OakNorth on 5 to 8 new active NACFB broker panels in London and South East. The broker who has OakNorth on their active list considers it on every qualifying deal — an average of 3 to 5 deals per year per broker.

  • 15 broker contacts built from NACFB directory
  • Angle: Monite as new capability + lending speed advantage
  • Goal: 5 new active broker panel relationships
Pillar 4

Existing Borrower Cross-sell

OakNorth has a 40%+ repeat borrower rate — thousands of existing lending relationships that have never been offered business savings. The cross-sell email to existing borrowers about the Business Earn Vault has near-zero CAC.

  • Email campaign to existing borrowers not yet using savings
  • Angle: Notice/Fixed accounts (3.62–3.80% AER) versus their current operating account rate
  • Goal: 10 to 15 deposit accounts opened from existing borrowers
Pillar 5

Authority Engine — CFO Content Layer

A LinkedIn content flywheel targeting SME CFO audience + a 20-week nurture sequence for deposit prospects who respond but are not yet ready. Converts OakNorth's attention into a 24-month pipeline.

  • LinkedIn posts targeting FSCS, finance automation, lending speed
  • 20-week nurture: no pitches, pure value delivery
  • Lead scoring: hot alerts to OakNorth commercial team
10-Day Activation Sequence

How the outbound channel goes from zero to first conversations in 10 days.

Day 1

Build targeting database

Companies House pull for SMEs in OakNorth's sectors with turnover £5m to £100m and cash surplus above £200k. LinkedIn CFO/FD resolution. Email verification via Hunter.io. Delivers 25-company Tier 1 list.

Day 2

NACFB broker list finalisation

15 NACFB member brokers in London and South East specialising in commercial loans and property for OakNorth's target deal size. Contact resolution and sequence loading.

Day 3

Deliver database to OakNorth

Tier 1 and Tier 2 databases delivered with sector tagging, cash surplus signals, FSCS switching probability score, and Companies House SIC classification.

Day 4 to 5

Deploy email_1 (Trigger)

"Deposits before Revolut does." — sent to 25 Tier 1 targets. FSCS change + OakNorth Notice 125-day 3.62% AER versus Barclays ~1.5%. Subject: 33 characters, no spam words.

Day 6

Reply triage

Categorise responses: interested (book discovery call), neutral (continue to email_2 day 10), no response (continue to email_2). Update campaign-ready.json.

Day 7 to 8

Deploy broker engagement outreach

Tier 2 list — Monite product capability as new recommendation for SME clients + lending speed as competitive advantage. Aim for panel inclusion, not an immediate referral.

Day 9

PVP check + Companies House lookup

Run Companies House new director appointments lookup against OakNorth's target SIC codes. If 10+ confirmed firms found, deploy pvp_email. If not, proceed to email_2 on day 10.

Day 10

Deploy email_2 (Friction)

"Allica's banker just had coffee with your broker." — Tier 1 non-responders. 80% broker dependence + Allica local banker threat. Different angle from email_1.
