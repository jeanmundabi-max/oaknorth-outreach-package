Three Windows Open Right Now

Each is time-limited. Each closes as the market resets habits over the next 6 to 18 months.

Window 1: The FSCS Tailwind (closes mid-2027)

The FSCS deposit protection limit rose from £85,000 to £120,000 on 1 December 2025. UK SMEs who previously spread deposits across multiple banks for FSCS protection now have structural permission to consolidate at a single challenger bank.

Annual advantage of OakNorth 125-day Notice over Barclays on £500,000: £10,600. At Fixed 12-month: £11,500. The case for switching is arithmetic, not emotional. No challenger bank is communicating this to SME decision-makers directly.

Window 2: Revolut's Banking Licence (ongoing competitive pressure)

Revolut received its full UK banking licence on 11 March 2026. FSCS-protected business savings are now available to 500,000+ existing Revolut Business customers who already trust the app. These are the same SME CFOs OakNorth wants as depositors. OakNorth's Easy Access rate (2.35% AER) likely beats Revolut's GBP savings product — verify at revolut.com before citing. But Revolut has the relationship and the app. Without direct outreach, Revolut converts those deposits passively.

Window 3: Monite Product Launch (Q2 2026 — imminent)

OakNorth acquired Monite (10 March 2026) to bring accounts payable, accounts receivable, and expense management natively into its banking interface. First capabilities launch Q2 2026 with no documented demand generation programme.

Indeed UK shows 23+ companies in OakNorth's target sector and size band posting for Accounts Payable or Accounts Receivable manager roles in the last 30 days. Each posting is a confirmed pain signal — the exact pain Monite eliminates. A Finance Director spending £35,000 to £50,000 on a headcount hire could instead switch to OakNorth and solve the same problem natively. The acquisition window is at product launch, not 6 months later when the market has formed habits.

How the Direct Acquisition Channel Compounds

Three Growth Feedback Loops — each reinforcing the next. The channel gets cheaper and stronger with every account opened.

Growth Loop 1

Direct Deposit Acquisition — Compounds

Companies House identifies CFOs with operating cash above £100k → Outbound email (FSCS yield argument) → CFO opens Business Notice Account → Named case study created → Case study becomes social proof on acquisition page → Next cohort converts at higher rate. CAC decreases per account as the proof loop closes.

Growth Loop 2

Broker Relationship — Compounds

NACFB broker engagement → OakNorth added to active panel → Broker considers OakNorth on 3 to 5 deals per year → OakNorth wins deal + cross-sells Business Notice Account to borrower → Case study from that relationship gives next broker a concrete reason to recommend. Each new panel position is worth 3 to 5 deals per year.

Growth Loop 3

Authority Engine — Compounds

LinkedIn CFO content flywheel → CFOs engage → 20-week value-only nurture sequence for not-yet-ready prospects → Lead scoring crosses hot threshold → OakNorth commercial team receives alert → Call begins where nurture left off → Converted CFO shares content with network. Organic reach compounds. Loop starts again.
