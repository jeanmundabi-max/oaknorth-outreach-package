How I Would Build OakNorth's Target List

Before this interview, I looked at who OakNorth's Business Banking product is right for — and how to find those people before the competition does. There are four groups worth targeting. They are not all the same. Here is how I would rank them, how to find them, and what to say to each one.

1
Priority 1 — Strongest Signal
Companies About to Hire Someone for Paperwork
Businesses posting jobs for finance admin staff — the Monite connection
Who They Are

These are businesses posting jobs for someone to manage their invoices and payments. They want to hire because their finance team is drowning in manual work — chasing invoices, matching receipts, approving payments by hand.

They have a confirmed problem right now. The job posting proves it.

Why OakNorth Can Help Right Now

OakNorth acquired a product called Monite in March 2026. Monite automates that same work — inside the business bank account.

Most of these companies do not know this product exists yet. That is the window.

How to Find Them
  • Go to job sites: Indeed, Reed, LinkedIn Jobs
  • Search for: "accounts payable manager", "purchase ledger", "finance systems manager"
  • Filter: UK companies, posted in the last 90 days, 10 to 200 employees
  • Check the company on Companies House — confirm they have been trading for 3 or more years
  • Find the Finance Director on LinkedIn — that is who to contact, not the person who posted the job
What to Say

"I saw you are hiring a Purchase Ledger Manager. OakNorth just released a product that automates that work inside your business account. Most companies hiring for this role have not heard about it yet. Happy to send details if useful."

⚡

Why this is Priority 1: These companies have a confirmed problem right now. The job posting is public proof. You are not guessing — they are advertising the problem themselves. That is the strongest outbound signal you can have.

2
Priority 2 — Good Timing Window
Companies Spreading Their Money Across Too Many Banks
Businesses that split deposits for FSCS protection — and no longer need to
Who They Are

These are businesses that split their cash across several banks. They did this to protect their money. The old UK deposit protection limit was £85,000 per bank. A company with £400,000 in cash would spread it across multiple accounts to stay protected.

Why This Changed

In December 2025, the UK raised the deposit protection limit to £120,000 per bank. These companies no longer need to split their money. But most have not simplified yet. They are still running 4 or 5 bank accounts for a reason that no longer applies.

How to Find Them
  • Use the Companies House API — it is free and public
  • Search for companies with £300,000 or more in cash on their balance sheet
  • Focus on sectors that hold more cash than average: law firms, accountants, consultants, tech companies
  • Find the Finance Director or CFO on LinkedIn — that is who makes this decision
  • Important: Companies House shows how much cash a company holds. It does NOT show which bank the money is in. Do not claim to know their bank.
What to Say

"The UK deposit protection limit went up to £120,000 in December 2025. A lot of businesses are still spreading their cash across several banks for protection they no longer need. OakNorth pays one of the highest rates in the market — over £10,500 extra per year on £500,000 compared to most high-street banks."

📅

Why this is Priority 2: The December 2025 rule change is the hook. Without a specific recent event like this, the outbound message is much harder to land. This window will not stay open forever.

3
Priority 3 — Right Message, Wrong Channel for Cold Outbound
Companies Losing Money Without Knowing It
Businesses with cash earning almost nothing at Barclays or HSBC
Who They Are

These are businesses with a lot of cash sitting in a current account at a big high-street bank earning very little. OakNorth pays much more. But these companies have not moved because nobody has shown them what the difference actually means in pounds per year.

Why Cold Email Does Not Work Here

There is no specific event that made this urgent today. The rate gap has always existed. A cold email without a trigger feels random. These companies respond when they are already searching for a better rate — not when you approach them out of nowhere.

How to Reach Them Instead
  • Comparison sites: Moneyfacts, Savings Champion, Raisin. Finance Directors go here when they have already decided to move their cash.
  • Paid search: Buy keywords like "best business savings account UK." Be first when they go looking.
  • Sector-specific landing pages: A healthcare Finance Director landing on a page written for healthcare businesses converts. A generic page does not.
What to Say

Nothing — let them come to you. The job here is to make OakNorth very easy to find when they are already looking. Be first on comparison sites. Be first in search results. Be on the page they land on when they search for a better rate.

🔍

Why this is Priority 3: The opportunity is real. The channel is wrong for cold outbound. Build the inbound channels first — comparison sites and paid search — and this segment comes in at the point when they are ready to act.

4
Priority 4 — Build Over Time
Companies That Grew Out of Revolut
Scale-up businesses that need a proper bank — not a fintech app
Who They Are

These are businesses that started on Revolut Business when they were small. Revolut worked well at £500,000 in revenue. Now they are at £3 million or £5 million and Revolut does not have what they need — no relationship banker, no lending, no credit decisions made by a human who knows their business.

What Changed in March 2026

Revolut received its full UK banking licence on 11 March 2026. This is making some business customers re-evaluate. If Revolut is now a real bank, should they be with a bank built specifically for businesses at their size? That question is open.

How to Reach Them
  • Content and search: Own the phrase "Revolut Business alternative UK." Write content explaining when a business outgrows a fintech account.
  • Why cold outbound does not work here: There is no public data that tells you which companies use Revolut Business. You cannot build a reliable target list.
  • The right move: Be the answer when they go searching. Comparison articles. Blog posts. LinkedIn content. That brings this segment in over time.
What to Say (In Content, Not Cold Email)

"You have outgrown your fintech account. You need a bank that grows with you — with real credit decisions, a relationship you can call, and deposit rates that make your cash work harder."

📈

Why this is Priority 4: It is a real and growing segment. But cold outbound will not work — there is no list to build. The answer is to be in the right place when they go looking. Build the content now so it compounds over the next 12 months.

Note for the interview: These four segments are ranked by how easy it is to find the right company, reach the right person, and give them a reason to act right now. Priority 1 and 2 are outbound — we go to them. Priority 3 and 4 are inbound — we make it easy for them to find us. A strong acquisition programme runs both at the same time. But it starts where the signal is clearest.
