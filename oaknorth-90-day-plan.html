Before I Started

What I Found Before I Arrived

I looked at OakNorth's acquisition funnel before this interview. Here is what I found.

Gap 1 — Website

No sector-specific pages

The website says the same thing to a healthcare Finance Director and a property developer. No landing page speaks to any one sector specifically.

Gap 2 — Copy

No CFO-voice messaging

The hero talks about OakNorth — not about what the Finance Director gets. The £10,500 extra per year on £500,000 is never surfaced on the page.

Gap 3 — Outbound

80% of lending leads: broker referrals

Deposits are almost entirely word of mouth. No systematic outbound sequence exists for reaching Finance Directors directly.

Gap 4 — Lifecycle

No account activation campaign

Accounts opened in 2023 to 2024 that have never placed a deposit. No sequence exists to move them to first operational use.

Gap 5 — Product Launch

Monite Finance Automation: Q2 2026 launch, no demand generation

The product is live. The campaigns are not.

Days 31–45

Fix the Website First

The single biggest quick win. The website says the same thing to every visitor.

Week 5 — Two Sector Pages

Healthcare + Professional Services

The two highest-volume sectors from the OakNorth loan book. Each page gets three things:

  • Interest rate differential calculator — £X left on the table at a high-street bank vs OakNorth
  • Sector-relevant proof — named customers or case studies where possible
  • FSCS protection callout — £120,000 since December 2025
Why This Matters

A CFO who lands on the page has a financial reason to act

Right now, every visitor sees: "Business banking for UK companies with ambition." That could be any bank. A healthcare Finance Director who lands on a page that says exactly what they get — in pounds per year — is a different conversation.

This is the fastest way to improve conversion from any channel we are already running.

I have already identified the content structure for both pages. Compliance review starts in week 5 — before any copy goes live.
Days 46–55

First Campaign Live

CFO outbound targeting sequence — using the FSCS consolidation trigger.

The Trigger

FSCS limit raised to £120,000 in December 2025

Many businesses split their cash across 4 or 5 banks for protection that no longer applies at the old limit. They no longer need to spread it. Most have not consolidated yet.

This is the most time-sensitive targeting window available right now.

The Sequence

2-touch email sequence, Finance Director only

  • Target: companies with £300,000+ cash on balance sheet (Companies House)
  • Focus: law firms, accountants, consultants, tech companies — sectors that hold more cash than average
  • Email 1: FSCS consolidation hook + OakNorth rate differential
  • Email 2: sector-specific proof point
  • Compliance sign-off: week 6 — before send
Day 55 First campaign is live.
Note Companies House shows cash balances. It does NOT show which bank holds the money. The outreach does not claim to know their current bank.

Days 61–75

Lifecycle Activation

Accounts opened in 2023 to 2024 that have never placed a deposit. This is the first activation campaign.

The Problem

Open accounts, no deposit

Acquisition brought them in. But they never placed their first operational deposit. These accounts exist. They are already OakNorth customers. Reactivation costs a fraction of new acquisition.

The JD specifically names this gap. It is a known problem. I have a plan for it.

The Campaign

Segmented sequence — FD voice, not founder voice

  • Pull every account opened 2023–2024 with zero deposits placed
  • Segment by company size and sector
  • Target the Finance Director — not the founder who opened the account
  • FD concerns: yield, admin simplicity, FSCS protection
  • Goal: 15% of dormant accounts place a first deposit within 90 days
Key insight: The founder picks the bank at the start. After that, the Finance Director manages the relationship. Every activation message must be in the FD's language — yield, rate, FSCS, admin simplicity. Not "we are a great bank."
Days 76–90

Introducer Programme Spec

80% of OakNorth lending leads come from NACFB broker referrals. Deposits have no equivalent structured programme. That is the gap.

What Exists

Informal broker relationships

NACFB brokers already refer lending clients. Some of those clients have deposits. But there is no programme that tracks this, rewards it, or systematically builds the relationship.

The relationship is there. The infrastructure is not.

What I Would Build

Spec, not the programme — delivered by day 90

  • Map existing NACFB broker relationships — top 20 by lending volume
  • Identify which relationships also touch deposit decisions
  • Build the business case: performance tracking, rate card, attribution, closed-loop reporting back to the broker
  • Present the spec to the Head of Marketing by day 90
  • Build the actual programme in Q2
I want to be honest: I have not built a formal introducer programme from scratch. I have worked with intermediary data at Santander — tracking which broker relationships produced the highest-value customers. The skills are the same. Building the infrastructure is the new part.
Days 76–90

Attribution Model

Connect every acquisition channel into one dashboard. Marketing-attributed vs BD-attributed vs broker-attributed — clearly separated.

What Gets Built

Channel-level CAC dashboard

  • Deposit volume by source (marketing / BD / broker / direct)
  • CAC per channel — what it costs to acquire each type of deposit
  • Activation rate — accounts opened vs accounts depositing
  • Campaign-attributed vs organic — separate views for marketing and commercial
Why This Matters

The same model I built at BT

At BT, Marketing, Finance, and Commercial were using different numbers in the same meeting. I built one shared view. The commercial team adopted it as their primary investment tool. That session is still running.

At OakNorth, the Head of Marketing should never have to ask what marketing is contributing. It should already be visible.

Tools: SQL for data, Salesforce CRM for pipeline data, Tableau or the analytics stack already in use at OakNorth. I adapt to whatever is in place — I do not rebuild what works.
