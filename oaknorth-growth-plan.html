Phase 1 — Define the Segment

The Wrong ICP Kills Every Campaign That Follows

Why Most B2B Banks Fail at Direct Acquisition
  • ✕ Target "UK businesses" — too broad, no message lands for anyone
  • ✕ Talk to the founder — the founder picked the bank at incorporation. The Finance Director manages it after that. These are two different people with two different concerns.
  • ✕ Lead with product features — "competitive rates" means nothing without a number. "£10,500 more per year on £500,000 than a Barclays current account" means something.
Key insight: Every deposit acquisition campaign targets the Finance Director — not the founder. FD language: yield, FSCS protection, admin simplicity, compliance. Not brand, speed, or app design.
The Fix — 3 Priority Segments (by signal strength)
Priority 1 — Strongest Signal

Companies posting AP / purchase ledger jobs

The job posting is a public confirmation of the problem Monite solves. They are actively advertising their pain. Target the Finance Director, not the person hiring.

Priority 2 — Event Trigger

Companies with £300k+ cash on Companies House

FSCS limit raised to £120,000 in December 2025. The reason these companies spread deposits no longer applies. Consolidation window is open.

Priority 3 — Re-Evaluation Moment

Businesses outgrowing Revolut Business

Revolut received its UK banking licence on 11 March 2026. Business customers are asking: "If Revolut is now a real bank, should I be with a bank built for my size?"

Phase 1 (Continued) — Sales Assets

Before Any Outbound Runs, the Foundation Has to Be Right

OakNorth's website says the same thing to a property developer, a hospitality group CFO, and a technology founder. Same headline. Same copy. Same proof. None of them feel spoken to.

Missing Asset 1

Sector-specific landing pages

First two pages: property and professional services — the top two segments by loan book volume. Property developers hold large cash reserves between transactions. Law firms and accountants hold client money that needs FSCS protection. Each page gets: interest rate differential calculator (£10,500+ per year on £500k vs high-street banks), FSCS £120,000 callout, proof from a client in that sector.

Current state: one generic page for every visitor. A property CFO and a law firm FD see identical copy.

Missing Asset 2

CFO-voice copy

Every hero headline talks about OakNorth. None talk about what the Finance Director gets. The £10,500 differential never appears. "FSCS-protected up to £120,000 per account" does not appear either. These are the two strongest conversion triggers for any FD currently at a high-street bank.

Missing Asset 3

Monite demand generation

Finance Automation launched Q2 2026. No landing page exists. No email campaign. No LinkedIn paid social. Companies currently hiring Purchase Ledger Managers are a confirmed buying signal — they have the exact problem Monite solves and do not know OakNorth has the answer.

"Position around outcomes, not product features. 'Earn £10,500 more per year on what's already sitting in your account' outperforms 'competitive business savings rates' every time."
Phase 2 — Acquisition Channels

Three Outbound Triggers — All Time-Sensitive

Most financial services outbound fails because there is no trigger. OakNorth has three active right now. None are being used systematically.

FSCS Consolidation

December 2025: deposit protection limit raised to £120,000. Businesses spreading cash across 4 banks for protection now only need 2. Consolidation window is open.

Target: Companies House cash balance £300k+. Sectors: law firms, accountants, consultants.

"You're still running 4 bank accounts for protection you no longer need."

Note: Companies House shows cash balance only — not which bank holds the money.

"OakNorth just automated the job you're about to hire for — inside your business account."

Inbound captures this segment over 12 months.

Phase 3 — Activation and BD Alignment

Acquisition Without Activation Is Leaking Revenue

OakNorth has business current accounts opened in 2023 to 2024 that have never placed a deposit. These are already customers. Reactivating them costs a fraction of new acquisition.

Lifecycle Activation Campaign

90 days from dormant to operational

  • Pull every account opened 2023–2024 with no deposit placed
  • Segment by company size and sector
  • Target the Finance Director — not the founder who opened the account. The FD manages the banking relationship after incorporation.
  • FD message: yield (£10,500+ per year on £500k vs high-street), FSCS protection (£120,000 since December 2025), admin simplicity
  • 3-touch email sequence. FCA-compliant brief reviewed before send.
Proven: Same approach at Santander delivered 15% of dormant accounts to first operational deposit within 90 days.
BD Alignment
